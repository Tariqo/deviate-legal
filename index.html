Privacy Policy

Effective July 15, 2026
Deviate is operated by Tariq, an independent developer. Questions and privacy requests can be sent to tariqali30021@gmail.com.

1. What stays on your device

Your daily mission catalogue, private Field Log, locally kept mission photos, and device-only profile information are stored on your device. Deviate does not request or use your location. Photos are re-encoded before online upload to remove embedded metadata.

2. Information used for online features

If you use online features, Deviate processes the information needed to provide them:

  • an authentication identifier and, depending on the sign-in method, an email or private relay address;
  • your display name, unique handle, companion icon, streak, online-name change date, and sharing preferences;
  • friend requests, accepted friendships, blocks, reports, check-ins, shared mission titles, and media-safety results;
  • profile and mission photos only when you choose to share them online; and
  • limited technical and security information processed by our hosting providers to deliver and protect the service.

We use this information to create your account, provide Friends and sharing features, protect users, enforce the Community Guidelines, prevent abuse, and comply with applicable law. We do not sell personal information, run third-party advertising, or use cross-app tracking.

3. Profiles, friends, and sharing

Exact-handle search can return a display name, handle, companion icon, and streak. Profile photos are available only to accepted friends. Blocked accounts cannot search for, request, or retrieve your social content.

Mission photos are shared only when you explicitly choose to include a photo. They are available to accepted, non-blocked friends through short-lived links. Shared mission records and files are removed by an automated process within the 24-hour sharing window. Profile photos remain until they are replaced or the online account is deleted.

4. Photo safety screening

Before your first outgoing online photo, Deviate asks for permission to send a re-encoded copy to Microsoft Azure Content Safety for automated screening for sexual, violent, hateful, and self-harm content. The photo is not published unless the screening succeeds.

Microsoft states that submitted images are not stored during detection, are not made available for human review by Microsoft, and are not used to train or improve the screening models. See Microsoft's data, privacy, and security information.

You can decline or withdraw this permission without losing other online features. Your companion becomes your online avatar, your profile photo remains on your device, and mission photos remain in your private Field Log. Friends can still see photo-free check-ins and shared mission titles, and you can still view approved photos shared by friends.

5. Service providers

  • Supabase provides hosted authentication, database, storage, and server functions for online features.
  • Microsoft provides automated harmful-content screening for photos you choose to share online.
  • Apple processes optional App Store tips and provides the associated transaction result. Deviate does not receive your payment-card details.

These providers process information on our behalf under their own security and legal obligations. Information may be processed in the regions used by these services and as permitted by applicable law.

6. Retention and deletion

Online profile and relationship data is kept while your online account exists or as needed to provide and secure the service. Shared mission media is removed within its 24-hour sharing window. Reports keep report metadata but do not preserve a separate copy of an expired mission photo. Some limited security or legal records may be retained where reasonably necessary.

You can delete your online account inside Deviate. This removes the hosted authentication identity, profile data, profile media, active shared media, requests, friendships, blocks, and reports created by that account. Device-only Field Log entries remain under your control and can be removed by deleting local app data or the app.

Turning off online features signs the device out and stops new social requests, but it does not delete the hosted account.

7. Your choices and rights

You can control photo sharing, withdraw or re-enable online-photo screening, block or remove friends, report content, turn off online features, and delete your online account from within the app. You may also ask to access, correct, or delete personal information by emailing us. Depending on where you live, you may have additional rights to object, restrict processing, receive a portable copy, or complain to a data-protection authority.

8. Legal bases

Where applicable, we process information to provide the service you request, based on your consent for optional online-photo screening, for legitimate safety and security interests, and where needed to meet legal obligations. You may withdraw photo-screening permission in the app at any time; withdrawal does not affect earlier lawful processing.

9. Children

Deviate is a general-audience app and is not directed specifically to children. If you believe a child has provided personal information in a way that is not permitted, contact us so we can investigate and take appropriate action.

10. Security and changes

We use access controls, private storage, short-lived media links, server-side permissions, and content-safety measures intended to protect online information. No system is completely secure. We may update this policy when the app or legal requirements change; the effective date above will show the latest revision.
